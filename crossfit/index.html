Hyperspace Project

The beginning stages at U of C

I implemented the Hyperspace project at the University Of Chicago Hospitals, with a budget of $1.2 million. The project was completed on time and under budget.

In this project we implemented Cadence and Prelude in Epic Hyperspace.

A secondary goal for this project was to remove, replace or eliminate most customization in these two (Cadence and Prelude) application. We removed over 95% of the original customizations, which greatly reduces the implementation cost of new versions of Epic going forward.

Cadence was utilized by over 1600 users and Prelude was accessed daily by over 170 users.

I managed a core team of 20 with 6 analysts, 5 programmers, 8 trainers and coordinated with infrastructure, interfaces and other internal teams.

We provided training for 1600+ users in 3 locations

Performed Unit Testing, Application testing and integrated testing, resolving all critical issues and developing a resolution or workaround for all medium and low priority issues before go live.

Managed go live support. Planned to provide support for 3 weeks and then reduces to less than two weeks due to the low number of issues generated during go live.

The support effort involved approximately 250 resources covering 3 hospitals and 7 clinics located through Chicago and Norwest Indiana.

EPICRX, RADIANT AND CLINDOCS ROLLOUT PROJECT

Middle stages of U of C

I managed the go live support phase of the EpicRx, Radiant and Clinical Documentation Phase I roll out at the University of Chicago Hospitals, with a budget of $300K + project specific budget for external resources.

Developed go live support plan to provide 3 weeks 24x7 support.

It involved:

  • Business command center at the hospitals where analysts were located
  • Technical command center in a remote location (Darien) where programmers and other technical resources were located,
  • Two meeting places in different hospitals for shift change meetings where the important issues identified during the shift were reviewed and shared with the next shift teams.
  • Overflow training rooms to provide additional user and superuser training when needed
  • Project Managers and IT executives’ meeting room
  • Sponsors’ meeting room

The support effort involved over 400 resources covering 3 hospitals.

The logistics included the setup of phone lines and network communication in both command centers. Set up of a Command center hot line accessible throughout the entire organization (all hospitals and remote locations).

Provided: parking, security, paging services, food and special attire for all 400+ participants.

Planned Cutover activities. Scheduled resources for cutover activities and support resources. Provided communications. Since this process occurred overnight, provided food, coffee and candies to keep people awake.

The data backfill, also included in the cutover plan, included the load of Radiology orders and EpicRx orders.

Also, planned and managed Dress Rehearsal with lessons learned session before go live. The lessons learned generated several documents that were produced by the trainers and distributed to users by the go live support team, significantly reducing the number of support call for the issues identified.

Epic ED

Managed the planning phase of the ED projects at the University of Chicago Hospitals, with a budget of $1.48 million.

The goal of this project was to integrate the Emergency Department system in the adult’s and children’s Emergency Departments (in two separated hospitals) with the rest of the organization’s systems and allow to easily track and admitting patients.

The core team built included 3 application coordinators, 2 workflow analyst, 2 trainers, one instructional designer, 3 superusers and several SMEs representing areas of the organization being impacted by the changes. The project had the Adult ED director as main sponsor assisted by two nurse champions and one physician champion. Developed project Scope, project Charter, project Plan, project Schedule, Risk matrix and risk response plan.

Revenue Cycle Assessment

Carefully disecting the correct course of action for every unique situation.

If you are like most healthcare organizations, you may decide to undergo a revenue cycle assessment when there are management changes, a new practice management system implementation, staffing adjustments, workflow augmentations or system and technology conversions. Sometimes it’s because you know that something’s not working and you need to identify it.

Over more than two decades, Hayes has conducted numerous revenue cycle assessments, and while each assessment is different, our methodology is consistent and proven. Each focused assessment provides a comprehensive analysis of your technology, processes, data and staff. And while our methodology provides the necessary stable foundation, it’s worth noting that each assessment is customized based on your distinct business needs, priorities and organization. So, you get the best of both worlds, stability and agility.

Our revenue cycle team offers three assessment options:

Rapid Assessment includes management interviews, benchmarking, opportunity identification, high-impact recommendations, and a technology utilization overview.

Deeper Dive adds operational interviews and observation, extended benchmarking, detailed technology/system analysis (setup), and further recommendations.

Focused Assessment provides recommendations in specific areas, such as the charge capture process, charge master setup, or billing components.


We are different from our competitors in that we believe in complete transparency. As part of our assessments, each of our clients receives a Performance Improvement Roadmap, which is a categorization of our recommendations based on level of impact, effort and cost. The Roadmap allows clients to easily determine which recommendations to implement first (high impact, low cost, low effort), which can be implemented internally, as well as those that require additional implementation support.

Interim Management

Finding the best team to carry out our goals.

For decades, Hayes’ has been partnering with healthcare organizations to bridge the gap from a vacant position to the hiring of a new, permanent employee. But that’s just one small part of our interim management support. We view each assignment as an opportunity to help set up your organization for future success, which is why we provide the necessary tools and insights at the end of each engagement.
